7 Wonders Of The Ancient World

A Piece Of Cake To Marvel At

They are a fascinating topic. The original Seven Wonders of the Ancient World are:
1. The Great Pyramid of Giza in Egypt
2. The Hanging Gardens of Babylon
3. The Statue of Zeus at Olympia
4. The Temple of Artemis at Ephesus
5. The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus
6. The Colossus of Rhodes
7. The Lighthouse of Alexandria
These wonders were considered remarkable architectural and engineering achievements of their time. They have captivated people's imaginations for centuries.

The Great Pyramid of Giza was the tomb of pharaoh Khufu, and still contains his granite sarcophagus. It had, like other tombs of Egyptian elites, four main purposes: It housed the body of the deceased and kept it safe. It demonstrated the status of the deceased and his family.
The Great Pyramid of Giza in Egypt! It's an absolute marvel. Did you know that it's the oldest and largest of the three pyramids in the Giza pyramid complex? It was built as a tomb for the Pharaoh Khufu during the Old Kingdom of Egypt, around 2560 BC. The pyramid stands at an impressive height of about 138 meters (455 feet) and was the tallest man-made structure in the world for over 3,800 years!
The Great Pyramid of Giza is known for its precise construction and incredible engineering. It's made up of an estimated 2.3 million stone blocks, each weighing several tons. The pyramid was originally covered in smooth limestone casing stones, but most of them have been removed over the years.

The Hanging Gardens of Babylon! They were one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. According to historical accounts, the gardens were built in the ancient city of Babylon, which is believed to be near present-day Iraq.he Hanging Gardens were said to be a remarkable feat of engineering and design. They were created by King Nebuchadnezzar II for his wife, who missed the lush greenery of her homeland. The gardens were designed as a series of terraces that rose high above the ground, supported by stone columns.
The gardens were adorned with a variety of plants and trees, creating a stunning oasis in the desert. Water was supplied through an intricate system of canals and pumps, allowing for the irrigation of the plants.
While there is no physical evidence remaining of the Hanging Gardens, historical accounts and descriptions have allowed us to imagine their beauty and grandeur. They continue to capture the imagination of people around the world.

The Statue of Zeus at Olympia was a giant seated figure, about 12.4 m (41 ft) tall, made by the Greek sculptor Phidias around 435 BC at the sanctuary of Olympia, Greece, and erected in the Temple of Zeus there. Zeus is the sky and thunder god in ancient Greek religion, who rules as king of the gods of Mount Olympus.

The Temple of Artemis at Ephesus! It's one of the seven wonders of the ancient world. This magnificent temple was dedicated to the Greek goddess Artemis and was located in the ancient city of Ephesus, which is now in modern-day Turkey. The Temple of Artemis was an architectural marvel, known for its grandeur and size. It was one of the largest temples in the ancient world, measuring about 377 feet long and 180 feet wide. The temple was adorned with impressive columns and intricate sculptures.Artemis, the goddess of the hunt and the moon, was highly revered in ancient Greek mythology. The temple served as a place of worship for her followers and was a center of religious and cultural significance. Unfortunately, the Temple of Artemis was destroyed multiple times throughout history, including by arson and invasion. Today, only the foundations and a few scattered ruins remain at the site in Ephesus.
